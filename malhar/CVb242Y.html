Notes on V +  बैठ  and the expression of misguided action

      Compound verbs are composed of the non-finite form of the main (or polar) verb followed by the inflected form of a vector. In (1)  भेज is the polar and  देना is the future imperative form of the vector  दे :
1.  गोबर को ऊख गोड़ने भेज देना ।
   'Send Gobar to hoe the cane.'
   (from Chapter One of   गोदान. See context.)
      The great majority of Hindi-Urdu compound verbs are made with one of the three vectors  जा,  दे, and  ले, which are homonymous with the main verbs for 'go', 'give', and 'take'. By using one of these three vectors speakers may specify the completeness and (where relevant) the physical orientation of the action expressed by the polar verb. Other, less common vectors, in addition to completeness and orientation, may be used to express the attitude of the speaker to the action denoted by the polar. Among these is  बैठ which is homonymous with the verb meaning 'sit' or 'sit down'. As a vector  बैठ implies misguided action. In example (2) the modifying clause  जो न करना चाहिए  makes this explicit:
2.  हाँ,  झुनिया पर उसे कभी- कभी अवश्य क्रोध अाता,  क्योंकि वह अज्ञान के कारण जो न करना चाहिए,  वह कर बैठती।
   'Yes, she would occasionally get angry with Juniya, because out of ignorance she would go and do things which she shouldn't.'
   (from Chapter Thirty-three of   गोदान. See context.)
Depending on tense and mood, the implication of misguided action may engender a connotation either of fear (3), of regret (4), or of derision (5):
3.  हीरा के सिर हत्या सवार है,  न जाने क्या कर बैठे ।
   'Hira's got murder on his mind. There's no telling what he might do!'
   (from Chapter Nine of   गोदान. See context.)
4.  लाखों कमाये,  मगर शामत अायी कि एक मेम से अाशनाई कर बैठे।  मुक़दमेबाज़ी हुई।  जेल जाते- जाते बचे।
   'He made millions, but had the misfortune of falling in love with an Englishwoman. The matter wound up in court. He almost went to jail.'
   (from Chapter Six of   गोदान. See context.)
5.  मालती ने उनकी लगाम खींची --  अच्छा,  अापको भी फ़लिासफ़ी में दख़ल है। मैं तो समझती थी,  अाप बहुत पहले अपनी फ़लिासफ़ी को गंगा में डुबो बैठे। नहीं,  अाप इतने बैंकों अौर कंपनियों के डाइरेक्टर न होते।
   'Mâlati decided to pull his chain. "Oh, so you, too, are an expert in philosophy. I thought you must have thrown your philosophy into the Ganges a long time ago. Otherwise, how could you be the director of so many banks and companies!"'
   (from Chapter Six of   गोदान. See context.)
These connotations render vector  बैठ particularly apt to appear in  कहीं-clauses expressing fear or anxiety:
6.  हरदम थर- थर काँपती रहती है कि कहीं धनिया कुछ कह न बैठे ।
   'She kept trembling with fear. What if Dhaniya were to say something (that she shouldn't)?'
   (from Chapter Eleven of   गोदान. See context.)
7.  उसे चिन्ता थी गोबर की।  लड़का लज्जाशील है,  अनाड़ी है,  अात्माभिमानी है,  कहीं कोई नादानी न कर बैठे।
   'He was worried about Gobar. The boy was introverted, he was naïve, he was egoistic. What if he were to do something foolish?'
   (from Chapter Ten of   गोदान. See context.)
 See related notes on  कहीं  and the expression of fear.
It is worth noting that compound verbs in  बैठ  that have the implication of misguided action almost always have [+human] subjects.  An apparent counterexample:
8.  चिड़िया का अपराध-भाव अब अाक्रमण का अाकार ले बैठा.ाम्प्ऌ.ाम्प्ऌ.ाम्प्ऌ
   'Chiriya's feeling of guilt took an aggressive form...'
   (from   चिड़िया अौर चील  by  सुषम बेदी.  See context.)
      Compound verbs in  बैठ  are generally made from transitive (rather than intransitive) polar verbs. However, because vector  बैठ  itself comes from an intransitive verb, compound verbs made from it do not allow  ने  with their subjects. See examples (4) and (5).
      Not every compound verb in  बैठ  has the implication of misguided action. There is a set of polar verbs whose compounds in  बैठ  suggest irresistibility (9) or undesirability (10):
9.  शराब का नशा उनके ऊपर सिंह की भाँति झपटा अौर दबोच बैठा।  वह कहते कुछ हैं,  मुँह से निकलता कुछ है।
   'The intoxication of drink pounced on him like a lion. He would say one thing and out of his mouth would come another thing.'
   (from Chapter Six of   गोदान. See context.)
10.  इसी तरह तुम लोगों ने किसानों को लूट- लूटकर मजूर बना डाला अौर अाप उनकी ज़मीन के मालिक बन बैठे।
   'This is how you people robbed the small-holders, turned them into landless laborers, and became yourselves the owners of their land.'
   (from Chapter Twenty-one of   गोदान. See context.)
The sequence  बन बैठ  in (10) is a commonly occurring fixed idiom meaning something like 'usurp'.
      When used with intransitive polar verbs like  चढ़ 'climb' and  उठ 'get up', vector  बैठ  retains much more of its literal meaning 'sit':
11.  होरी उनकी छाती पर चढ़ बैठा अौर ज़ोर से दाढ़ी पकड़कर खींची।
   'Hori sat on his chest and grasping his beard pulled on it with all his strength.'
   (from Chapter Six of   गोदान. See context.)
12.  सहसा गोबर चौंककर उठ बैठा अौर अाँखें मलता हुअा बोला --  अरे !  यह तो भोर हो गया।
   'Gobar suddenly started and sat up. Rubbing his eyes he said, "Hey! It's already morning!"'
   (from Chapter Four of   गोदान. See context.)
This last may actually be not a compound verb at all but a sequence of two full verbs, the first denoting a prior action, the second a later one. Compare the  उठ बैठा  in (12) with the  उठकर बैठ गये  in (13), both meaning 'sat up (in bed)':
13.  मेहता को ऐसा जान पड़ा,  उन कोमल हाथों ने जैसे सारा दर्द खींच लिया। उठकर बैठ गये अौर बोले -- दर्द तो दोपहर से ही हो रहा था अौर ऐसा सिर- दर्द मुझे अाज तक नहीं हुअा था,  मगर तुम्हारे हाथ रखते ही सिर ऐसा हल्का हो गया है मानों दर्द था ही नहीं।
   'To Mehta it seemed as if those delicate hands drew out all the pain. He sat up and said, "I have been in pain since the afternoon and I have never in my life had a headache like this one. But as soon as your hands touched me my head felt light again, as if there hadn't been any pain at all."'
   (from Chapter Thirty-three of   गोदान. See context.)
